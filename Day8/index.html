About Us

Rates

Karnataka, the sixth largest state in India, was ranked as the third most popular state in the country for tourism in 2014.[1][2] [3] It is home to 507 of the 3600 centrally protected monuments in India, second only to Uttar Pradesh.[4] The State Directorate of Archaeology and Museums protects an additional 752 monuments and another 25,000 monuments are yet to receive protection.[5]

Features

1.Hampi (Vijayanagara): UNESCO World Heritage site known for sprawling ruins, temples, and unique boulder-strewn landscapes.
2.Mysore (Mysuru): Cultural capital famous for the magnificent Mysore Palace, Dasara festivities, and royal heritage.
3.Coorg (Kodagu): "Scotland of India," featuring coffee plantations, misty hills, and waterfalls like Abbey Falls.
4.Bengaluru (Bangalore): Cosmopolitan capital with modern nightlife, Lalbagh Botanical Garden, and Cubbon Park.
5.Chikmagalur: "Coffee Land of Karnataka," home to the state's highest peak, Mullayanagiri, and scenic trekking trails

Popular Sites

While Bangalore and Mysore are major draws, Coorg (Kodagu), known as the "Scotland of India," often emerges as Karnataka's most visited destination, beloved for its coffee plantations, misty hills, waterfalls, and serene atmosphere, frequently combined with Mysore. Other top spots include the UNESCO site of Hampi, Mysore Palace, and the adventure hub of Dandeli, with Bangalore itself also a huge attraction.
